 Heat is a form of energy that flows from a body at a higher temperature to one at a lower temperature, due to the temperature difference between them. It is measured in joules (J) in the SI system. Temperature is the measure of the degree of hotness or coldness of a body, and it determines the direction of heat flow. Unlike heat, temperature is not a form of energy but an indicator of thermal state.
Heat is a function of the total kinetic energy of the particles in a substance, while temperature is proportional to the average kinetic energy of the particles. Heat depends on mass, specific heat capacity, and temperature change, while temperature is independent of mass. The instrument used to measure temperature is a thermometer.
Heat energy can cause:
Temperature Change â€“ Increases or decreases particle kinetic energy.
Change of State â€“ Melting, boiling, condensation, etc.
Expansion â€“ Increase in size (linear, area, volume).
Change in Density â€“ Volume increase leads to lower density.
Chemical Changes â€“ Causes reactions or decomposition.
Example: Ice melts to water, metals expand in heat, hot air rises due to lower density.

Heat transfer is the movement of thermal energy from one body to another due to temperature difference. The three main modes are: conduction, convection, and radiation.
Conduction occurs in solids when heat is transferred through particle vibrations and free electron movement without overall movement of the substance. Metals are good conductors due to free electrons, while wood and rubber are insulators.
Convection occurs in fluids (liquids and gases) by the bulk movement of particles. It is responsible for sea breezes, boiling water circulation, and convection currents in the atmosphere.
Radiation is the transfer of heat through electromagnetic waves without a medium. It can occur in a vacuum. Black, dull surfaces are good absorbers and emitters, while shiny surfaces are poor absorbers and good reflectors.
Applications include thermos flasks, heat exchangers, solar heaters, and insulation materials.
